FORECASTER DISCUSSION
NWS HONOLULU · HFO
Issued 12:00 PM HST Tue Apr 21 2026
OFFICIAL
Trade winds hold, but a disturbance is watching from the northeast.
A persistent upper-level ridge is keeping the islands in warm, classic trade wind weather today and Wednesday. The atmosphere is textbook — models agree, confidence is high, and the main story is the expected shower enhancement on windward slopes each afternoon. That changes Thursday, when a mid-level disturbance arrives to complicate things.
KEY POINTS
  • Trade winds 15–25 mph sustained, with gusts to 35 mph through the Ko'olau passes. The Wind Advisory for Oahu Windward remains in effect until 6 PM.
  • Afternoon shower activity will focus along windward slopes and the Ko'olau Range from 3–8 PM. Brief, locally heavy bursts possible — not all-day rain.
  • Today's high of 86°F is 4° above the April normal of 82°F. A warm, above-average air mass is in place.
  • Thursday's disturbance is the wildcard. GFS keeps it weak and fast-moving; ECMWF deepens it slightly. The spread in model solutions means Friday could be significantly wetter than advertised — or not.
MODEL DISCUSSION
  • GFS and NAM are in close agreement through Wednesday evening — the ridge is strong, trade flow is locked in.
  • GFS has the Thursday disturbance tracking northwest of Oahu, limiting impact. ECMWF takes a slightly more southern track that would enhance showers significantly. The NBM is threading the needle between the two.
  • Confidence drops significantly beyond Thursday. The 72-hour forecast should be treated as higher uncertainty than typical.
⚡ WHAT TO WATCH
Monitor Thursday's 5 AM discussion for updated track and confidence on the disturbance. If ECMWF's solution verifies, expect a Flash Flood Watch to be considered for windward Oahu Friday. Lightning is possible with any heavier showers after 4 PM today.
Forecaster: J. Reyes · NWS Honolulu
